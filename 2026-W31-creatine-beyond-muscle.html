Creatine: it was never just for muscle

5g a day, decades of safety data, and a growing case file outside the weight room.

Hey crew, Max here.

Creatine is the most-studied supplement in sports nutrition — and also the one your grandma should probably know about. The lifting evidence is rock solid; the brain, mood, and recovery evidence is younger, messier, and genuinely interesting.

Three things to take away

🥇 5g/day is the boring, correct dose — no loading phase needed unless you want results in a week instead of a month.

🥈 Outside lifting, signals are real but modest: small cognitive bumps under stress, depression-adjunct hints, early concussion-recovery data.

🥉 Vegetarians, sleep-deprived people, and older adults seem to benefit most — their baseline creatine stores are lower to begin with.

COGNITION
🧠 What the brain meta-analysis actually showed
The Rae et al. lineage
The 2003 Rae trial kicked it off — 5g/day for 6 weeks improved working memory and processing speed in vegetarians. Later meta-analyses softened the story but didn't kill it.

A 2018 meta-analysis pooling cognition trials found small but real improvements in short-term memory and reasoning, especially under stress (sleep deprivation, hypoxia, mental fatigue).

Well-rested, well-fed omnivores on a normal day? The effect basically washes out. Creatine seems to help when the brain's energy budget is already squeezed.

Honest read on the cognition data
  • ✅ Sleep-deprived brains benefit most. — Trials with 24h+ sleep loss show the cleanest cognitive rescue.
  • ✅ Vegetarians get bigger bumps. — Lower baseline stores = more room to top up.
  • ⚠️ Effect sizes are modest. — We're talking 'measurably better on a test,' not 'feels like Adderall.'
  • ❌ Don't expect a healthy-person miracle. — If you sleep 8 hours and eat steak, the ceiling is lower.
🔗 Read more
Rae et al., Proc. Biol. Sci. 2003; subsequent meta-analyses in Exp Gerontol & Neurosci Biobehav Rev.
MOOD
🌧️ The depression-adjunct trials
The signal worth watching
Small RCTs adding 3–5g creatine to SSRIs have shown faster and larger reductions in depression scores than SSRI alone — particularly in women.

The mechanism makes sense: depressed brains show impaired frontal-lobe energy metabolism, and creatine props up cellular ATP. It's a metabolic adjunct, not a mood drug.

But — and this matters — most trials are small (n<60), short, and single-site. The signal is consistent enough to take seriously, not big enough to oversell.

Where the evidence sits
Promising adjunct, not a standalone. The big confirmatory trials haven't been run yet.
🔗 Read more
Lyoo et al., Am J Psychiatry 2012; Kious et al., Nutrients 2019 review.
CONCUSSION
🏈 Mild TBI: the most interesting frontier
The pediatric data
In a trial of children with TBI, 0.4g/kg/day of creatine reduced post-concussive headache, fatigue, and dizziness over 6 months versus controls.

Concussion is, mechanistically, an energy crisis — ion pumps go haywire, ATP demand spikes, mitochondria struggle. Creatine plausibly helps by buffering that.

The catch: the strongest data is from one research group, in kids, over a decade ago. Adult mTBI evidence is mostly preclinical. Replication is overdue.

How to read the mTBI evidence
  • 🧪 Mechanism is plausible. — Creatine supports the exact ATP system concussions disrupt.
  • 📊 Human data is thin. — Mostly Sakellaris et al., pediatric, small samples.
  • 🎯 Low downside. — Decades of safety data at 3–5g/day in adults make it a reasonable consideration alongside standard care.
🔗 Read more
Sakellaris et al., J Trauma 2006; Acta Paediatr 2008.
FATIGUE
🔋 Sleep-deprived, jet-lagged, long-COVID-tired
Where the fatigue signal shows up
  • 😴 Sleep deprivation: yes. — 20–25g/day for a week before sleep loss preserved cognitive performance in pilots and shift-work models.
  • 🦠 Post-viral fatigue: maybe. — Early long-COVID pilots (Slankamenac et al.) showed reduced fatigue scores at 4g/day — small, unblinded, hypothesis-generating only.
  • 🏃 Recovery between sessions: solid. — Reduced muscle damage markers and faster strength recovery is one of the better-replicated non-strength findings.
  • ⚠️ Chronic fatigue syndrome: unclear. — Mixed, mostly underpowered. Don't expect transformation.
McMorris et al., Psychopharmacology 2006; Slankamenac et al., Food Sci Nutr 2023.
HOT TAKE
💬 The honest summary nobody puts on the label
Max's take
Creatine is one of the cheapest, safest, best-studied molecules in the supplement aisle — and its non-muscle effects are real but modest. Anyone selling it as a nootropic, antidepressant, or brain-injury cure is running ahead of the data. Anyone dismissing it as 'just for lifters' is running behind it.
Editorial — based on the body of evidence cited above.
HEALTH HACK OF THE WEEK
🥄 5g monohydrate, daily, with any meal. — Forget loading. Forget HCl, buffered, 'micronized elite.' Plain creatine monohydrate is the form with the evidence — and it's pennies a day.
💧 Drink water like a normal human. — Creatine pulls a little water into muscle cells. That's a feature, not a side effect. Stay hydrated and stop panicking about the scale.
📅 Give it 3–4 weeks before judging. — Muscle and brain creatine stores saturate gradually at 5g/day. The 'I don't feel anything in 3 days' crowd quit before the curve.
