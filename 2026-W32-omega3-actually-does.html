Omega-3s in 2026: the trials that moved the needle, and the ones that quietly didn't

VITAL, REDUCE-IT, STRENGTH. Three big trials, three different answers. Here's what survives the hype filter.

Hey crew, Max here.

Fish oil is the supplement aisle's most stubborn celebrity — wildly popular, weirdly under-scrutinized by the people who take it. The big cardiovascular outcome trials are now mature enough to grade. Time to separate the EPA story from the DHA story from the marketing story.

Three things to take away

🥇 Mixed EPA+DHA fish oil at low doses doesn't move hard cardiovascular outcomes — VITAL and STRENGTH both said so.

🥈 High-dose icosapent ethyl (pure EPA, 4 g/day) in REDUCE-IT cut MACE by 25% — but the mineral oil placebo controversy still hangs over it.

🥉 The clearest wins are in pregnancy and early childhood, not in your 55-year-old cardiologist's clinic.

THE LANDSCAPE
🐟 Three trials, three verdicts, one mess
The 30-second version
VITAL: null. STRENGTH: null. REDUCE-IT: positive but contentious. Same molecule family, completely different results — because dose, form, and placebo matter more than the bottle suggests.

VITAL randomized ~25,000 adults to 1 g/day of EPA+DHA (Omacor-style) and found no significant drop in the primary composite of major cardiovascular events over ~5 years. A clean, well-powered null.

STRENGTH tested 4 g/day of a carboxylic-acid EPA+DHA blend (Epanova) in ~13,000 high-risk patients — and was stopped early for futility. Same dose as REDUCE-IT. Different molecule. Different outcome.

Reading the three trials without the spin
  • 🧪 Dose isn't the only variable. — REDUCE-IT and STRENGTH both used 4 g/day. Only one was positive.
  • ⚗️ Form matters. — Icosapent ethyl is purified EPA. STRENGTH used a mixed EPA+DHA carboxylic acid. VITAL used low-dose ethyl ester.
  • 🛢️ Placebo controversy is real. — REDUCE-IT used mineral oil, which raised LDL and hs-CRP in controls — inflating the apparent benefit, per FDA reviewers.
  • 🎯 Population matters. — REDUCE-IT enrolled statin-treated patients with high triglycerides. VITAL was primary prevention. Different game.

THE NUMBER
📉 The REDUCE-IT result everyone quotes
What REDUCE-IT measured
Icosapent ethyl 4 g/day cut the primary composite endpoint (CV death, MI, stroke, revascularization, unstable angina) by 25% vs. mineral oil placebo (HR 0.75).

Real number, real trial, ~8,000 statin-treated patients with elevated triglycerides. The catch: when STRENGTH ran a similar dose with a corn-oil placebo and a mixed EPA+DHA preparation, the effect vanished entirely. That's either a placebo problem, a DHA problem, or both.

Bhatt et al., NEJM 2019; Nicholls et al., JAMA 2020
EPA vs DHA
⚔️ Why purified EPA and EPA+DHA may not be the same drug
Where the two molecules diverge
  • 🩸 EPA lowers triglycerides; DHA does too — but raises LDL more. — DHA can nudge LDL-C up 3–5%, partially offsetting cardiovascular benefit.
  • 🔥 EPA has stronger anti-inflammatory and membrane-stabilizing signals. — It incorporates into plaques and may slow progression on imaging (EVAPORATE substudy).
  • 🧠 DHA dominates the brain. — It's the structural omega-3 in neurons and retina. That doesn't mean supplementing it fixes adult cognition.
  • 🧬 EPA:DHA ratio in trials varies wildly. — From pure EPA (REDUCE-IT) to roughly 1:1 (STRENGTH) to whatever's in your costco bottle. They're not interchangeable.
  • 🚨 Both raise atrial fibrillation risk at high doses. — Meta-analyses now show a consistent ~25–35% increase in AFib with 1–4 g/day. This is the real downside no one's marketing.
Meta-analyses: Circulation 2021; EHJ 2021
THE OTHER ENDPOINTS
🧠 Cognition: mostly disappointing. Pregnancy: actually convincing.

For adult cognition and dementia prevention, the trial record is rough. VITAL's cognitive substudy showed no benefit. Multiple RCTs in older adults: null on memory, executive function, and global cognition. Observational links keep showing up; randomized data keeps saying no.

Pregnancy and early life is where omega-3s actually look like a real intervention. The ORIP and DOMInO trials, plus a 2018 Cochrane review of ~20,000 pregnancies, show DHA supplementation reduces early preterm birth (&lt;34 weeks) by roughly 42%. That's a hard endpoint with real consequences.

Where the signal is actually strong
  • 🤰 Preterm birth reduction. — Cochrane 2018: ~11% lower risk of any preterm birth, ~42% lower for early preterm. DHA ~500–1000 mg/day.
  • 👶 Modest infant visual and neurodevelopmental signals. — Smaller effects than the marketing suggests, but consistent across trials.
  • 🧓 Adult cognition: don't hold your breath. — RCTs in cognitively healthy and MCI populations have largely failed to show benefit.

HOT TAKE
🎣 What I actually do, and what I tell people who ask
Max's take
If you're not pregnant and not on a high-dose icosapent ethyl protocol for sky-high triglycerides, the case for swallowing a daily fish oil capsule for your heart is weak. Eat 2–3 servings of fatty fish a week. Skip the gummies. Watch for AFib if your doctor is loading you with grams of the stuff.

HEALTH HACK OF THE WEEK
🐟 Eat the fish, don't chase the capsule. — Two servings of salmon, sardines, mackerel, or herring per week gets you in the dose range where epidemiology actually looks decent — without the AFib risk that comes with multi-gram supplementation.
🧾 If you supplement, know your form. — Check the label for EPA and DHA in milligrams, not 'fish oil 1000 mg.' Most cheap bottles are ~30% omega-3 by mass and oxidize fast. Refrigerate them.
🩺 High triglycerides? That's a real conversation. — Icosapent ethyl is the only omega-3 product with a positive outcomes trial behind it — and even that's contested. It's a clinical decision, not a supplement-aisle one.
