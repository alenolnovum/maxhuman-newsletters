Omega-3s in 2026: which trials moved the needle, and which ones quietly died

VITAL flopped. REDUCE-IT stunned. STRENGTH muddied the water. Here's what the EPA-vs-DHA fight really tells us.

Hey crew, Max here.

Fish oil has had a brutal decade in the evidence base. Three megatrials, three different answers, and a supplement aisle that still acts like none of it happened — so let's pull the receipts and see what actually survived peer review.

Three things to take away

🥇 VITAL and STRENGTH were largely null for cardiovascular events — generic EPA+DHA capsules at 1 g/day did not move hard outcomes.

🥈 REDUCE-IT was positive but contested: 4 g/day icosapent ethyl (EPA-only) cut MACE by 25% — though the mineral oil placebo debate still hasn't fully died.

🥉 Pregnancy and early childhood are where omega-3s actually shine — preterm birth reduction is the cleanest signal in the entire literature.

THE BIG THREE TRIALS
🐟 VITAL, REDUCE-IT, STRENGTH — three trials, three verdicts
The 30-second version
Generic fish oil failed. High-dose EPA-only worked (with an asterisk). Mixed EPA+DHA at high dose did nothing.

VITAL (n=25,871, 1 g/day EPA+DHA) was essentially null for the primary composite of major cardiovascular events. A modest MI signal showed up in secondary analyses, but the headline result was a flat line.

REDUCE-IT (n=8,179, 4 g/day icosapent ethyl) hit hard: a 25% relative reduction in MACE in statin-treated patients with elevated triglycerides. STRENGTH (n=13,078, 4 g/day mixed EPA+DHA carboxylic acid) at almost identical dose — nothing. Which is the entire puzzle.

What the data actually supports
  • ✅ High-dose EPA-only in high-risk statin patients — REDUCE-IT's population — established CVD or diabetes plus TGs 135–499 mg/dL — is the only group with a positive RCT.
  • ⚠️ The mineral oil placebo problem — STRENGTH used corn oil; REDUCE-IT used mineral oil, which raised LDL and hsCRP in the placebo arm. Critics argue this inflated the apparent EPA benefit.
  • ❌ Generic 1 g fish oil for primary prevention — VITAL and meta-analyses through 2024 do not support a hard-outcome benefit at this dose in low-to-moderate risk adults.
  • 🤷 DHA's role in CV outcomes — Honestly unresolved. STRENGTH's failure suggests DHA might blunt EPA's effect, but the trial designs differ enough that 'might' is doing heavy lifting.
The honest read
If you're a REDUCE-IT-eligible patient, icosapent ethyl has a real case. If you're a healthy 40-year-old with a costco fish oil habit, the evidence for hard outcomes is basically zero.

ONE NUMBER
📉 The REDUCE-IT headline that won't stop being argued about
The result that broke the consensus
25% relative risk reduction in the primary MACE composite with 4 g/day icosapent ethyl vs mineral oil placebo, over a median 4.9 years.
Bhatt et al., NEJM 2019 (REDUCE-IT)
EPA vs DHA
⚖️ Where the two fatty acids actually diverge
Same family, different behavior
  • 🧬 EPA sits in membranes differently — EPA appears to stabilize membrane cholesterol domains and reduce oxidation; high-dose EPA monotherapy is what REDUCE-IT tested.
  • 🧠 DHA dominates neural tissue — DHA is structurally enriched in brain and retina — which is why cognitive and pregnancy hypotheses centered on it, even when CV trials didn't.
  • 🩸 DHA raises LDL-C more than EPA — Consistent finding across trials. Mechanistically plausible, clinically real, and probably part of why STRENGTH didn't deliver.
  • 🔥 Both lower triglycerides — This is the one thing nobody disputes. 20–30% TG reduction at 4 g/day is reproducible across formulations.
  • ❓ Whether DHA cancels EPA's CV benefit — The honest answer is we don't know. The STRENGTH vs REDUCE-IT divergence is the field's most uncomfortable open question.
Mason et al., ATVB 2020; Mozaffarian & Wu, Circulation Research 2018
HOT TAKE
🧠 The cognitive trials nobody wants to talk about
Max's take
Despite a decade of hype, omega-3 trials for dementia prevention and cognitive decline have been overwhelmingly null — VITAL-COG, AREDS2 cognitive substudy, and the DHA-focused trials all failed to show meaningful benefit. Beautiful mechanistic story, lousy trial record.
VITAL-COG (JAMA 2020); Quinn et al. (DHA in AD, JAMA 2010); AREDS2 cognitive function
WHERE OMEGA-3s ACTUALLY WIN
🤰 Pregnancy and early childhood — the cleanest signal in the literature
The result that actually replicates
Cochrane meta-analysis: omega-3 supplementation in pregnancy reduces preterm birth before 37 weeks by ~11% and early preterm (<34 weeks) by ~42%.

This is the one area where the evidence is boring in the best way: multiple large RCTs, consistent direction, plausible mechanism, and a Cochrane review that didn't have to torture the data to find a signal.

The ORIP and DOMInO trials muddied the dose-response question (very high DHA may slightly *increase* late-term risk), but the core preterm-birth reduction has held up. This is where the supplement aisle is actually justified — just not for the reasons most people buy it.

Middleton et al., Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2018; ORIP trial NEJM 2019
PRACTICAL CALL
🐟 Eat the fish, skip the random softgels. — 2–3 servings/week of fatty fish gives you EPA+DHA in a food matrix with a far better evidence base than generic 1 g capsules for hard outcomes.
💊 If you're REDUCE-IT-eligible, the conversation is different. — Established CVD or diabetes + elevated TGs on a statin? Icosapent ethyl at 4 g/day has actual RCT support — that's a clinician conversation, not a Amazon decision.
🚫 Stop buying fish oil for your brain. — The cognitive trial record is bad. If you want one defensible reason to supplement, it's pregnancy — not 'longevity.'
